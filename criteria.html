A weekend creating electronic art

Greenwich Peninsula, London

16th-17th May 2015

Prizes and Awards



Introduction

The Art Hackathon is not a competition. The purpose of it is for people with diverse interests and personalities to collaborate, be creative with art and technology and above all have fun and enjoy themselves. That said, to add spice to the weekend’s proceedings we’re awarding two prizes of no monetary value based on the criteria listed below. The first award is the people’s choice award and the second is the award handed by a jury of artists, developers and curators.



Criteria


Successful implementation of the theme


We’re looking for artworks that show that the team have explored the idea well and have come up with an original response to the specified theme. We’re not necessarily looking for something that fits the criteria of the theme, but is the most inspired interpretation of it.


Originality and inventiveness


We want to find things that are awesomely intriguing, pieces that couldn’t have emerged in any other environment where the creativity wasn’t bouncing off the walls. We’d like to see creations that are manifestations of ideas that haven’t been seen before.


A finished product


As much as we want people to explore and experiment, we want them to get making as well. Creations score extra points for creating something that they can demonstrate to judges and other participants at the end.


Group dynamics and collaborative spirit


Let’s not forget the primary reason for being here. We’d like to see teams that work well and individuals that are eager to share ideas and resources with other teams too.
