A weekend creating electronic art

Greenwich Peninsula, London

16th-17th May 2015

Anthropological research



Why?


To help us understand the process of the hackathon as well as its material outcomes, we will be conducting qualitative research. This will give us insight on how teams form and collaborate throughout the event, both formally and informally. We will use this research to improve future hackathons, both future iterations of this one and those run by other organisers.



How?


The Art Hackathon team will be collecting photos, video and audio of you at work on the day and feedback after the event. Please also feel free to contribute your own experiential observations in the form of tweets, photos, blog posts, etc, either by using the hashtag #arthackathon or by emailing research@arthackathon.co.uk. Anything tagged to #arthackathon or which is publicly available about the art hackathon will be included in a Storify of the day's experience, which we will share.

You may also contribute to the optional Art Hackathon Diaries project. If you sign up for this, you must contribute a diary entry three times each day--the times will be assigned and you will get a reminder. The diaries can be about anything you like and they can be any length that you like; simply share your thoughts as soon as you are reasonably able to in the diary when you get the reminder. You can also contribute longer or more entries if you wish to do so. Diary data will be anonymized if we quote from it publicly, but you are free to quote your own diary anywhere you like.



Then What?

Our resident ethnographer will be analysing all the qualitative research materials that we collect. We will definitely write a blog post about what we learned; there may also be a more formal white paper that we publish.



Privacy

You may choose to withdraw from the research at any time. For anything you published on a public platform, simply adjust your privacy settings on the post and they'll disappear from our Storify. To withdraw from the diaries project, email research@arthackathon.co.uk or arthackathon.research@gmail.com and we will delete your data.
