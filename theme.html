A weekend creating electronic art

Greenwich Peninsula, London

16th-17th May 2015

Art Hackathon theme



Why a theme?


Although it may seem liberating to have a no-theme hackathon, experience shows that a few boundaries actually help people come up with ideas (creativity loves constraints). The Art Hackathon has a theme, but people are allowed to interpret it as they like, or even ignore it if they have some idea they're passionate about. This event's theme is...


deceit


"The action or practice of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth. Deception violates relational rules and is considered to be a violation of expectations."

Methods of deceit:
  • camouflage
  • illusion
  • distraction
  • concealment
  • dissimulation
  • propaganda
  • sleight of hand
The five primary forms of deception are:
  • Lies: making up information or giving information that is the opposite or very different from the truth.
  • Equivocations: making an indirect, ambiguous, or contradictory statement.
  • Concealments: omitting information that is important or relevant to the given context, or engaging in behavior that helps hide relevant information.
  • Exaggerations: overstatement or stretching the truth to a degree.
  • Understatements: minimization or downplaying aspects of the truth.


Examples of projects using some form of deceit:


  • The Cage is an installation that uses projection to invite the public to interact with physical bird cage to trigger the release of projected birds (auroraMeccanica).
  • Newstweek: A device for manipulating news read by other people on wireless hotspots. Built into a small and innocuous wall plug, the Newstweek device appears part of the local infrastructure, allowing writers to remotely edit news read on wireless devices without the awareness of their users. By the same artist, the Transparency grenade is presented as an iconic cure for the lack of government and corporate transparency. Equipped with a tiny computer, microphone and powerful wireless antenna, the Transparency Grenade captures network traffic and audio at the site and securely and anonymously streams it to a dedicated server where it is mined for information (Julian Oliver).
  • Perspective: challenges how heavily a person's perception of information is based on the channel through which they receive it. Using projection mapping, speech synthesis and computer animation, Barack Obama "delivered a contemporary speech" that was in fact subverted subject matter in the form of an historic Vladimir Putin speech (artists: Robert Wollner, Adam John Williams).
  • The Pirate Cinema snoops on torrent downloads in the name of art and flashes clips from downloaded video segments onto giant projector screens in real time, producing an effect similar to the ending of the classic 1983 flick "War Games." (artist: Nicolas Maigret)
  • Politwoops displays all the deleted tweets of politicians in real time.
